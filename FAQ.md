# FAQ

## 1. What is Isekai?

Isekai is an unofficial desktop clock and planner for Sky: Children of the Light. It helps track Sky-time events, countdowns, goals, collection progress, and other planning details from a fast local app.

## 2. Why use an app when Sky clock websites already exist?

Websites are useful, but a desktop app is faster to open, easier to keep nearby, and does not depend on a browser tab. Isekai also includes an overlay, so you can quickly check timers while doing something else without switching to a website.

## 3. What makes the overlay useful?

The overlay is a small always-on-top window designed for quick access. You can keep key Sky timers visible without keeping the full app open in front of your other windows.

## 4. Is this app official?

No. Isekai is an unofficial fan-made project. Sky: Children of the Light belongs to thatgamecompany.

## 5. Does the app connect to my Sky account?

No. The app does not log in to your Sky account, read account data, or interact with game servers.

## 6. Is my data private?

Yes. Planner data, goals, wishlist state, and settings are stored locally on your device. The app is designed so your personal app data stays on your machine.

## 7. Does Isekai send data anywhere?

No personal planner or settings data is sent from the app. The app uses local storage for your data and does not need telemetry to work.

## 8. Is the app open source?

Yes. The project is open source, so anyone can inspect the code, build it, and verify how it works.

## 9. Is Isekai safe to use?

Isekai is built as a local desktop utility. It does not need Sky account credentials, does not modify the game, and stores user data locally. As with any app, download it only from the official project release page.

## 10. Does the app modify Sky: Children of the Light?

No. Isekai is not a mod, cheat, injector, or game client tool. It is a separate desktop clock and planner.

## 11. Will using Isekai affect my Sky account?

No. Since Isekai does not connect to your account or modify the game, it should not affect your Sky account.

## 12. Is the app lightweight?

Yes. Isekai is designed to be extremely lightweight and fast, including on low-end devices. The app uses Tauri, Rust, React, and a small desktop bundle instead of a heavy browser-based shell.

## 13. How much RAM does it use?

The target is to stay very small, around less than 10 MB of RAM for lightweight use where possible. Actual memory usage can vary by operating system, app state, and release version.

## 14. How much storage does it need?

The goal is to keep the installed app small, around 20 MB of storage or less where possible. Exact size can change by build, platform, and included update files.

## 15. How big is the installer?

The installer target is small, around 8 MB for compact releases. The exact installer size can vary between versions.

## 16. Does Isekai support auto updates?

Yes. The app includes updater support so releases can be updated without manually reinstalling every time.

## 17. Does the app work offline?

Most app features are designed to work locally. Event calculations, planner data, themes, and saved settings can be used without relying on a live website.

## 18. Why is a desktop app faster than a website?

A local app can launch directly, keep data nearby, and avoid repeated browser setup, tabs, extensions, and page loading. It also lets features like the overlay work more naturally.

## 19. What Sky time zone does the app use?

Isekai tracks Sky-time aware events using the America/Los_Angeles time zone, which matches the timing used for Sky reset and recurring schedules.

## 20. What can I plan with the app?

The app includes local planner storage for goals and wishlist state. It is meant to help you track what you want to do, collect, or remember while playing.

## 21. Does it have themes?

Yes. Isekai supports light, dark, and system theme modes.

## 22. Is it good for low-end devices?

Yes. The app is intentionally built to be small and responsive, making it suitable for low-end laptops, older PCs, and devices where a heavier app would feel slow.

## 23. Why not just keep a browser tab open?

A browser tab can work, but it is easy to lose among other tabs and can use more memory. Isekai gives you a dedicated app, local planner state, and an overlay that stays accessible while you use other windows.

## 24. Does the app need an internet connection?

The app does not need internet for local timers and saved planner data. Internet may be needed for downloading updates or getting a new release.

## 25. Can I verify the security claims myself?

Yes. Because the project is open source, you can inspect the code, review the storage behavior, and build the app yourself.

## 26. Does Isekai collect analytics or telemetry?

No. The app is intended to be a local utility and does not require analytics or telemetry collection for its core features.

## 27. Who is this app for?

Isekai is for Sky players who want a fast desktop companion for event timers, planning, collection tracking, and quick overlay access without relying only on a website.
